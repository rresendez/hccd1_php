Hoehn Rural Drainage Development Program

Hoehn Rural Drainage Development Project is part of the Rural Development Program for Hidalgo County Precinct No. 4.  The area benefiting from the proposed improvements is located in a flood prone area which makes it susceptible to flooding even with moderate rain.  This project consists of installing a drainage network of pipes ranging in size from 24” to 54” along Hoehn Drive that are designed to transport storm water that collects in the roadside ditches via grated inlets to discharge into the J-08/La Puerta Drain Ditch.  The roadside ditches will be re-graded and culvert pipe will be installed where it is necessary to insure positive flow to the proposed drainage structures.  The existing detention pond south of Hoehn Drive Subdivision currently collects the storm water from the subdivision; this water is then pumped via an electrical pumping station to the same Hidalgo County Irrigation District No. 1 ditch that the proposed improvements outfall into.  To prevent water from backing up into the detention pond when the water surface elevation gets higher than the receiving ditch, a flood gate will be installed on the proposed 54” pipe that connects to the outfall.  A manhole structure has been installed immediately up-stream from the gate structure that will allow for the pumping of the 54” line with a 12” portable pump in case the gate has to be closed.  The road crossing at Mon-Mack Road was improved to allow for better flow in the system.  In addition to the improvements performed by the contractor, HCDD No. 1 Engineering and Maintenance & Operations Divisions designed and improved the ditch from Hoehn Road to the North Main Drain III which is approximately ¼ mile east of McColl Road.  HCDD No. 1 created a bench system that will facilitate maintenance and at the same time create more volume in the ditch.

Project is 100% complete as of December 2016.

Contractor: Texas Cordia Construction, LLC.
