Mission Inlet Recertification

L & G Engineering, Inc.

 

Updated as of:

2012 Bond Project List No.

April 2017

7

The Mission Inlet project existing conditions H&H modeling was completed early in 2014 and results were utilized to complete the proposed conditions H&H modeling.  The Phase II Alternative Analysis was completed late this past year (signed and sealed technical report) and includes various scenarios of improvement measures such as Outlet Structure & Pump Station Expansion, Proposed Detention Basin Locations, & Channel Improvement & Realignment (incl. Levee/Berm Relocations, Overbank Fill, etc.).  The final selected improvements included an upgrade of the outlet structure (add six–10x10 boxes and two pumps) and an expansion of the channel to a wider benched section at the locations generally identified in the attached exhibit.  The major tasks include the development of finalized graphic files and schematic highlighting the proposed improvements for use in future final PS&E and development of Conditional Letter of Map Revision (CLOMR) and associated documentation required for coordination with FEMA based on the proposed improvements.

Based on an existing conditions analysis, approximately 670 acres of land can be released from FEMA "A" Zones.  This land is located west of Cimarron County Club along the Mission Inlet.

In the proposed condition (additional outlet structures, additional pumps, and channel improvements) approximately 830 additional acres of land can be released for a total of 1500 acres.

Study was completed in 2014.  Currently design plans are being developed (60% complete) for the proposed condition improvements needed to remove approximately 830 acres from a flood zone. Coordination is on-going with local entities and the Conditional Letter of Map Revision will be submitted to FEMA once final plans are developed.
