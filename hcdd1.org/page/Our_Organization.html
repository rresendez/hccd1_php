Personal
Originally from Matamoros, Mexico, Mr. Raul E. Sesin, PE, CFM currently resides in Edinburg, Texas. He moved from Mexico to Tuscaloosa, Alabama at the age of 8 where he learned a new language and continued his education all the way through college receiving his Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering from the University of Alabama. Mr. Sesin, has three daughters, Dianaly 10, Dayana 11 and Zoe Isabella 20, who currently is a senior at the University of Texas-Rio Grande Valley.

Engineering & Planning Experience
In February 2015, Mr. Sesin, was appointed as General Manager of the Hidalgo County Drainage District No. 1 bringing with him 24 years of experience which includes: design, project coordination, residential and commercial development, site inspection, construction management, utility and drainage projects. His experience consists primarily of project and manpower scheduling, client relations, preparation of plans, specifications, estimates, and feasibility studies within South Texas. For 14 years Mr. Sesin, oversaw the permitting process for commercial and residential development within the un-incorporated areas of the County as the Hidalgo County Planning Administrator. During this time he worked with State agencies and testified before State Legislature for the development and continued enforcement of Colonia prevention laws.

Objective and Goals as HCDD No. 1 General Manager
As Hidalgo County Drainage District No. 1 General Manager, Mr. Sesin’s first priority has been to evaluate the current District operation to ensure accountability and transparency to the tax payer and the general public.  Mr. Sesin will continue to work with local, state and federal agencies to obtain funds for current and future projects to accomplish drainage goals for Hidalgo County and the region.

Under the leadership of Mr. Sesin, there has been over 22 in-house projects initiated and 17 have been completed with an estimated construction cost of about $7.7 million dollars worth of improvements. Additionally, there is approximately $3.7 million dollars worth of work still under construction, for a total of $11.4 million dollars worth of work that has been designed by in-house District Engineers. These projects address both regional drainage needs and rural development needs for Hidalgo County.
